Amanda, the nutritionist, says:

“He seems to like sweets for breakfast. Instead of a full sugar cereal, try to get the cereal with 1/3 (or reduce) sugar. If you can’t find a lower sugar version of the cereal, mix frosted flakes with corn flakes to cut down on the sugar. You an always mix a high sugar cereal with a high fiber cereal to get added bang for your buck but still have a tasty breakfast that kids would like to eat. Tacos are also a great way to get vegetables into the meal. Watch out for the sour cream and cheese (if you use them choose the low fat options – they cost the same at the grocery store). And be careful with the tortillas…they can add a lot of calories fast!”
